Mosaic Jello

Mosaic Jello is a classic Mexican dessert. Regular jello in different flavors mixed with a creamy jello mixture made into a beautiful bundt mold.

Ingredients

The beauty of this recipe, is that you have complete creative control on how you want your jello dessert to look. Use as many jello colors as you want!

  • 3 to 4 different flavor jello boxes, 3oz each box.
  • Unflavored Gelatin.
  • 1 can condensed milk.
  • 1 can evaporated milk.
  • 1 can media crema or regular milk.
  • Vanilla extract.
  • Oil spray or vegetable oil.

Steps

  1. Make the different jello flavors according to the instructions on the box. Do this the night before so the jello has time to solidify.
  2. Spray the mold with oil before pouring your mix into it. This helps the jello come out easily. If you do not have spray oil, spread it lightly with a napkin making sure that there are no lumps of oil left in the mold.
  3. Once the jello is very solid, cut it into squares with knife and lift from mold using a spatula or a flat spoon. Carefully and gently mix all of the jello flavors in a large bowl.
  4. Now make the gelatine: add the 4 envelopes (28 oz total) in a ½ cup of cold water. Mix well with a fork. Let it rest for at least 5 minutes or until it's solidified. Let it rest for at least 5 minutes or until it is solidified.
  5. In a separate medium to large bowl, add the evaporated milk, the condensed milk and the media crema (or regular milk) and 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract. Mix with a whisk until incorporated well.
  6. Microwave the gelatine for 40 to 45 seconds to soften up then add to the milk mixture. Whisk to incorporate well.
  7. Coat a 12-cup bundt cake mold (or your preferred mold) with cooking with cooking spray. Start adding a layer of jello and layer of milk. Repeat the layers until you finish all ingredients.
  8. Cover with aluminum foil and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  9. To succesfully invert the bundt jello onto a plate, start by placing the plate upside down on top of the jello mold. Hold the plate and mold securely together and flip them over simultaneously. Gently tap the bottom and sides of the mold to loosen the jello. Carefully lift the mold, and the jello should release onto the plate.
